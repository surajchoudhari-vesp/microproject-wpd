Bibliography

  • Encyclopaedia Britannica. Articles on the History of Law, Code of Hammurabi, Magna Carta, and Roman Law.
  • United Nations. Universal Declaration of Human Rights and modern human rights principles.
  • The British Library. Historical information on the Magna Carta (1215) and medieval legal traditions.
  • A History of Law. Overview of the development of legal systems from ancient to modern times.
  • Law in the Ancient World. Information about ancient legal systems including Mesopotamian, Greek, and Roman law.
  • Montesquieu. (1748). The Spirit of the Laws. A foundational work discussing Enlightenment ideas about government, separation of powers, and law.
  • Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy. Articles on natural rights, Enlightenment philosophy, and legal theory.
  • Dicey, A. V. (1915). Introduction to the Study of the Law of the Constitution. London: Macmillan.
  • Bingham, T. (2010). The Rule of Law. London: Penguin Books.
  • Wacks, R. (2014). Law: A Very Short Introduction. Oxford: Oxford University Press.
  • Kritzer, H. M. (2002). Legal Systems of the World. Santa Barbara: ABC‑CLIO.
  • Hammurabi. (c. 1754 BCE). The Code of Hammurabi.
  • Aristotle. (c. 350 BCE). The Athenian Constitution.
